# Lijphart/Przeworski/Verba Datasets {lpvdata}

This package makes available datasets that have won the Lijphart/Przeworski/Verba Award for publicly available datasets in comparative public policy.

The full list of datasets can be found, here: [apsanet.org](https://www.apsanet.org/STAFF/Membership-Workspace/Organized-Sections/Organized-Section-Awards/Organized-Section-Awards/Section-20#dataset)


| Year | Type | Dataset | Description |
|------|--------|---------|---------|
| 2022 | Winner | [Quota Adoption and Reform Over Time](https://www.openicpsr.org/openicpsr/project/100918/version/V1/view) | Longitudinal information about the adoption, implementation, and reform of national gender quotas across the world |
| 2021 | Winner | [Who governs?](https://politicscentre.nuffield.ox.ac.uk/whogov-dataset/download-dataset/) | Information on governing elites from 1966 to 2021 in all countries |
| 2021 | Honour | [Measuring Immigration Policies](https://www.migrationdataportal.org/blog/impic-new-and-more-comprehensive-way-measure-immigration-policies) | The IMPIC project provides a set of indices to measure immigration policies across time, countries and policy fields. |
| 2020 | Winner | [European Social Survey](https://www.europeansocialsurvey.org) | Survey of changing public attitudes and values within Europe |
| 2020 | Honour | [Chapel Hill Expert Survey](https://cadmus.eui.eu/handle/1814/69975) | Estimating national party positioning on European integration, ideology and policy issues for national parties |
| 2019 | Winner | [The Comparative Agendas Project](https://www.comparativeagendas.net) | Collection of data from archived sources to track policy outcomes across countries. |
| 2019 | Honour | [The Mass Mobilization in Autocracies Database](https://mmadatabase.org/get/) | Sub-national data on mass mobilization events worldwide, both anti- and pro-regime protest at the level of cities with daily resolution. |
| 2018 | Winner | [AmericasBarometer](http://datasets.americasbarometer.org/database/index.php?freeUser=true) | Measures voters attitudes and experiencesin the Americas of economy, rule of law, state capacity, trust in institutions, individual values, corruption, security, and more |
| 2018 | Honour | [Digital Whistlerblower](https://digiwhist.eu/about-digiwhist/) | Micro-level data of individual public procurement transactions, winning firms’ finance and ownership structures to detect conflict of interests |
| 2017 | Winner | [Global State Revenue and Expenditures Data Set](https://gsre.info) | Includes 39 indicators covering major aspects of state finance for 161 countries from 1946 to 2006 as well as nine indicators of public and government employment. |
| 2016 | Winner | [Varieties of Democracy](https://github.com/vdeminstitute/vdemdata) | Distinguishes between five high-level principles of democracy: electoral, liberal, participatory, deliberative, and egalitarian, and collects data to measure these principles. |
| 2015 | Winner | [The Autocratic Regimes Data Set](https://sites.psu.edu/dictators/) | Autocratic regime start/end dates, regime types, level of violence during regime failure event, categorization of regime failure events & a typology of autocracies and non-autocracies |
| 2014 | Winner | [Archigos: A Data Set on Leaders 1875-2004](http://ksgleditsch.com/archigos.html) | Dataset with information on leaders in 188 countries from 1875 to 2004 |
| 2013 | Winner | [Comparative Constitutions Project](https://comparativeconstitutionsproject.org) | Data on the formal characteristics (form and content) of written constitutions, both current and historical, for most independent states since 1789 |
| 2012 | Winner | [Constituency-Level Elections Archive](https://electiondataarchive.org/data-and-documentation/clea-lower-chamber-elections-archive/) | Detailed election results at the constituency level for lower chamber and upper chamber legislative elections from around the world. |
| 2011 | Winner | [Uppsala Conflict Data Program](https://www.pcr.uu.se/research/ucdp/about-ucdp/) | World’s main provider of data on organized violence and the oldest ongoing data collection project for civil war, with a history of almost 40 years |
| 2010 | Winner | [Arab Barometer](https://www.arabbarometer.org/survey-data/data-downloads/) | Survey data of more than 26,000 MENA citizens including COVID19, the economy, government trust, gender norms, status of women, environment etc. |
| 2009 | Winner | [The Quality of Government Dataset](https://www.qogdata.pol.gu.se/data/qog_std_cs_jan22.csv) | Data consists of approximately 2100 variables from more than 100 data sources related to Quality of Government. |
| 2008 | Winner | [Comparative Welfare Entitlements Dataset](http://cwed2.org/download.php) | Data set provides systematic data on institutional features of social insurance programs in 33 countries spanning much of the post‐war period |
| 2007 | Winner | [Data set on European Protest and Coercion](https://ronfran.ku.edu/data/index.html) | Data on protest and coercion including date, day, action type, location, protest group and targets. |
| 2006 | Winner | [CIRI Human Rights](http://www.humanrightsdata.com/p/data-documentation.html) | Dataset contains quantitative information on government respect for 15 internationally recognized human rights for 202 countries  from 1981-2011 |
| 2005 | Winner | [Luxembourg Income Study](https://www.lisdatacenter.org/data-access/key-figures/) | Income database of harmonised microdata collected from c50 countries in Europe, North America, Latin America, Africa, Asia, and Australasia from1980 to 2019 |
| 2004 | Winner | [The Afro-barometer](https://www.afrobarometer.org/survey-resource/merged-round-7-data-34-countries-2019/) | Survey data relating on Africans’ views on democracy, governance, and other issues |
| 2003 | Winner | [Manifesto Project](https://github.com/ManifestoProject/manifestoR) | Provides parties’ policy positions derived from analysis of parties’ electoral manifestos. Covers over 1000 parties from 1945 until today in over 50 countries. |
| 2002 | Winner | [ACLP Political and Economic Database](https://sites.google.com/site/joseantoniocheibub/datasets/aclp) | Provides data on democracy and development. Covers 135 countries, from 1950 or year of independence to 1990. |
| 2001 | Winner | [World Values Survey/Eurobarometers Data Set](https://www.worldvaluessurvey.org/wvs.jsp) | Survey data from almost 100 countries using a common questionnaire on economic development, democratization, religion, gender equality, social capital, and subjective well-being |
| 2000 | Winner | [Comparative Study of Electoral Systems](https://cses.org) | Survey data on elections together along with voting, demographic, district and macro variables allowing analysis of voting behaviour from a multilevel perspective |
| 1999 | Winner | [Polyarchy](https://sites.nd.edu/michael-coppedge/?page_id=26) | Data relating to 125 variables in the years 1985 and 2000 relating to freedom of expression, freedom of organisation, media pluralism and fair elections from Robert Dahl. |
| 1999 | Winner | [Civic Culture Data Set](https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/web/ICPSR/studies/7201/datadocumentation#) | Survey data from Germany, Italy, Mexico, the UK on attitudes toward bureaucracy, police, political parties, campaigning, as well as school, family, and place of work |
